Cole, 2005

When Cole Hill, of Springfield,MO was born, he suffered from a severe meconium aspiration. The stress of his birth caused him to inhale a bowel movement.  His lungs were completely full of meconium.  He was rushed to St. Louis where the doctor’s were not sure that he was going to live. After 17 days in St. Louis, Cole made a complete recovery!  Your donations to Children’s Miracle Network helped provide Cole with synagis shots to prevent him from contracting the deadly RSV virus.

Lauren, 2006

When Lauren Anderson of Springfield, MO was born, she suffered a Bilateral Brachial Plexus Injury.  The nerves that are responsible for arm and hand movement were severed and stretched.  This led to partial paralysis in both arms.  Lauren has undergone many surgeries and therapies to increase movement in her hands and arms. Your donations to Children’s Miracle Network allow Lauren to get Physical and Occupational Therapy at CoxHealth, pay for her growth hormone treatment, and help her family get to specialty appointments that are out of town.

Blake, 2006

When Blake Shanks of Willard, MO was born, he was diagnosed with a heart defect called Tetralogy of Fallot, which meant he had a hole in his heart and his pulmonary valve was too narrow.  If he cried he lost oxygen and turned blue. When Blake was only 4 months old, he underwent open heart surgery to correct the defect.  He is now doing wonderful!  Your donations to Children’s Miracle Network help Blake’s parent’s travel to his specialty appointments out of town, provided him with oxygen when he needed it, and assisted with the cost of his CoxHealth hospital bills.

Danielle, 2007

When Danielle Roeder was 6 months old, she started losing all muscle control until she was completely wheelchair bound.  She could not move a muscle and when she spoke it was in a tiny strained whisper.  The doctor’s did not know what was wrong with her and she was misdiagnosed for 6 years.  During the summer of 2005, with the help of Children’s Miracle Network, Danielle and her family traveled to the Mayo Clinic and hoped for a miracle.  After days of testing, Danielle was diagnosed with L-Dopa Responsive Dystonia which mimics the symptoms of Parkinsons.  Now, with daily medication, Danielle is able to drive a power wheelchair, talk, sing, write, and most importantly walk.

Megan, 2008

On Mother’s Day of 2007, Megan White was diagnosed with Stage 4 cancer.  Megan underwent surgery to remove a 6 pound tumor that had swallowed one of her kidneys.  She then spent the next year undergoing extensive chemotherapy treatment.  Through all of this, Megan continued to show her courage and strength.  Her greatest love in life is dancing and throughout her battle, she continued to dance and even compete at national competitions.  Thanks to your donations, Megan is now able to call herself a cancer survivor.

Jodelyn & Scotlyn, 2008

Jodelyn and Scotlyn Belew were born 13 weeks premature. Jodelyn weighed 2 pounds 1 ounce and Scotlyn weighed a mere 1 pound 14 ounces.  Though Scotlyn was smaller at birth, she has grown into a beautiful healthy little girl.  Jodelyn suffered a staph infection in her brain and underwent a craniotomy at 6 months of age. She was diagnosed with Cerebral Palsy shortly after. Jodelyn recieves physical and occupational therapy at the CoxHealth Meyer Center and is growing stronger everyday.

Jackson, 2009

At just 2 years old, Jackson was diagnosed with a rare form of cancer called rhabdomyosarcoma. The cancer was found in his prostate and bladder. Jackson received radiation and chemotherapy in St. Louis. Children’s Miracle Network has helped Jackson’s family with the cost of travel during his treatments and also with CoxHealth hospital and urgent care bills. About a year later, Jackson’s family received the good news that he was officially cancer free. Jackson is now 3 years old and is doing GREAT! He is enjoying being a normal three year old boy and his parents are grateful for the donations made to Children’s Miracle Network to help their son have a second chance.

Brooke, 2010

In 2005, when Brooke Kincaid was 2 years old, her parents noticed a lump in her leg and took her to the ER at Cox South. Doctors found another lump in her skull and on her adrenal gland. After being rushed to St. Louis Children’s Hospital, Brooke was diagnosed with Stage 4 Neuroblastoma. After 6 rounds of chemo therapy and a bone marrow transplant, Brooke is able to call herself a cancer survivor.

Kadie, 2011

When Kadie was born she was immediately flown to St. Louis after doctors discovered that her heart was on the wrong side of her body and she was missing most of her esophagus. In St. Louis, Kadie was diagnosed with a rare condition called Scimitar Syndrome and has had 11 surgeries.  She continues to overcome medical obstacles and is truly a miracle. She enjoys cooking, gymnastics and karate.
